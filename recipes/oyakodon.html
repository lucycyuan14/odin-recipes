Oyakodon

DESCRIPTION

Prepare your taste buds for a delightful journey with Oyakodon, a Japanese culinary masterpiece. This traditional dish showcases the perfect harmony of tender chicken, fluffy eggs, and a flavorful sauce. As you take each bite, you'll experience the essence of Japanese cuisine unfolding on your palate. The succulent chicken, gently simmered in a savory broth, blends seamlessly with the silky eggs, creating a symphony of textures that is both comforting and satisfying. Served over a bed of steamed rice, Oyakodon embodies the balance of flavors that Japan is renowned for, making it a true delight for any food enthusiast.

INGREDIENTS

  • 1/2 onion (peeled)
  • 10 oz boneless, skinless chicken thighs (typically 1-2 thighs)
  • 1 tablespoon sake (for marinating the chicken)
  • 3-4 large eggs

For the Seasonings

  • 1/2 cup dashi
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons mirin
  • 2 teaspoons sugar

For Serving

  • 2 servings cooked Japanese short-grain rice
  • 4 green onion/scallion shoots (chopped)
  • Japanese seven spice (optional)
  • Japanese sansho pepper (optional)

STEPS

To Prepare the Seasonings

  1. Combine 1/2 cup dashi, 2 tablespoons soy sauce, 2 tablespoon mirin, and 2 teaspoons sugar in a bowl or a liquid measuring cup and mix all together until the sugar is dissolved.

To Prepare the Ingredients

  1. Slice 1/2 onion lengthwise, about 1/4 inch wide.
  2. Chop 4 shoots of green onion/scallions 1/2 inch wide.
  3. Trim off the extra bits of fat and connective tissue from 10 oz boneless, skinless chicken thighs with a knife.
  4. Cut the chicken thigh along the grain into strips 3/4-1 inch wide. Next, angle your knife back and diagonally, and then slice the chicken strips against the grain into pieces about 3/4-1 inch square.
  5. Transfer the chicken into a bowl or tray and sprinkle with 1 tablespoon sake. Set aside for 5 minutes.
  6. Crack 3-4 large eggs into a bowl. Using a pair of chopsticks and whisk the eggs together.

To Cook the Oyakodon

  1. With the stove off, add the sliced onions to a medium frying pan in a single layer. Then add the seasonings mixture. It should just cover the onions.
  2. Turn on the heat to medium and bring it to a simmer. Once simmering, add the chicken on top of the onions.
  3. Make sure the onions and chicken are evenly ditributed. Once simmering again, lower the heat to medium low. Cook, uncovered, for 5 minutes or until the chicken is no longer pink and the onions are tender. Halfway through, flip the chicken.
  4. Now increase the heat to medium. With the cooking liquid simmering, drizzle the egg mixture in a circular pattern over the chicken and onions.
  5. Add the chopped green onions/scallions on top and cook on medium low until the egg is done to your liking.
  6. Serve steamed rice in bowls. Spoon the cooked chicken and egg mixture onto the steam rice and drizzle the desired amount of pan sauce on top.
