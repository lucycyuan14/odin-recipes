Beef Noodle Soup

DESCRIPTION

Prepare yourself for a culinary adventure with Taiwanese Beef Noodle Soup-a medley of flavors that will awaken your taste buds. Picture succulent beef, slow-cooked to perfection, swimming in a fragrant broth that's infused with aromatic spices and herbs. The noodles, with their satisfying chewiness, eagerly soak up the flavors, creating a harmonious balance in every mouthful. From the first sip to the last slurp, this iconic dish showcases the essence of Taiwanese cuisine and invites you to savor its irresistible charm.

INGREDIENTS

  • 3 pounds beef shank (1.4 kg, cut into 2-inch chunks)
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • A 2-inch piece of ginger (smashed)
  • 6 cloves garlic (smashed)
  • 3 scallions (cut into 2-inch segments)
  • 1 onion (cut into wedges)
  • 1 tomato (cut into wedges)
  • 4 dried chilies (ripped in half)
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons spicy bean paste (douban jiang)
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup Shaoxing wine
  • 8 cups water (to make the soup broth)
  • 1 Chinese aromatic herb packet (see below to create your own spice sachet)
  • 32 ounces fresh wheat (white) noodles
  • A small handful of bok choy for each serving
  • Cilantro (finely chopped)
  • Scallions (finely chopped)
  • Picked mustard greens (to taste)

To create your own space sachet, place the following ingredients in a cheesecloth

  • 4 star anise
  • 1 Chinese cinnamon stick
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 1 tablespoon fennel seeds
  • 1 tablespoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander seeds
  • 2 tablespoons Sichuan peppercorns
  • 1/4 teaspoon five spice powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

STEPS

  1. Boil enough water in a pot to boil all of your beef. Once the water is boiling, add the beef. Let it come back up to a boil, and boil for 1 minute. Strain in a colander and rinse thoroughly with fresh water to remove any impurities
  2. Next, add the oil, crushed ginger, garlic, scallions, and onions in that order in a large pot on medium heat. Stir to lightly caramelize. Let the onion turn translucent. Add the tomato and dried chilies.
  3. Next, add the meat to the pot. Then add the tomato paste, spicy bean paste, sugar, soy sauce, and Shaoxing wine, and mix thoroughly.
  4. Pour 8 cups of water into the pot. Add the spice packet.
  5. Close the lid of the pot and cook the soup on a low simmer for 3-4 hours.
  6. Once the soup is ready, boil some noodles per package instructions, and in the last minute or two of the noodles cooking, throw your bok choy in and blanch until just tender.
  7. Serve each bowl of Taiwanese beef noodle soup with a serving of noodles, a few stalks of bok choy, and generous sprinklings of finely minced cilantro, scallions, and Chinese picked mustard greens.
